🔵 What is Legora?

Legora is an AI legal assistant built for law firms of all sizes — it handles contract review, legal research, document drafting and due diligence. Its AI is trained on legal documents and case law, making it more accurate on legal tasks than general AI tools. Legora is designed to be deployed quickly with minimal IT overhead, making it accessible to mid-size and smaller firms.

🟡 What is Harvey AI?

Harvey AI is the premium enterprise legal AI platform — built on GPT-4 with extensive legal fine-tuning and used by some of the world's leading law firms including Allen & Overy, Macfarlanes and PwC Legal. It handles complex legal research, contract analysis, due diligence and regulatory work at scale. Harvey is deeply customisable for enterprise deployment and compliance requirements.

⚡ Bottom Line

For most law firms, Legora is the more practical starting point — faster to deploy, more accessible pricing and strong enough for the majority of everyday legal AI tasks including contract review, research and drafting. Harvey AI is the choice for elite firms with enterprise requirements, large transaction volumes and the budget and procurement capacity to match. If you're not sure, start with Legora — you can always graduate to Harvey as your AI adoption matures.

Frequently Asked Questions
Both are strong for contract review — Legora is purpose-built for it and accessible to firms of all sizes, making it the practical choice for most contract-heavy practices. Harvey has an edge for extremely complex, high-stakes transactions at large firms where deep customisation and enterprise-scale processing matter. For everyday contract review, Legora is excellent and more straightforward to deploy.
Both Legora and Harvey are built with legal-grade data security — attorney-client privilege protection, data isolation and SOC 2 compliance. Harvey has an edge for the most demanding enterprise security requirements due to its deeper enterprise customisation and the scrutiny it has faced deploying at Am Law 100 firms. Both are significantly more secure than using consumer AI tools like ChatGPT for sensitive client work.
Harvey AI is primarily positioned for large law firms and enterprise legal teams — its pricing and procurement process is typically enterprise-scale. Small and mid-size firms generally find Harvey difficult to access and cost-prohibitive. Legora, Clio Duo or tools like CoCounsel (Thomson Reuters) are better fits for smaller firms. That said, Harvey's positioning continues to evolve, so it's worth checking directly if you're interested.
Beyond Legora and Harvey, the main legal AI tools worth evaluating are: CoCounsel by Thomson Reuters (strong research, trusted brand), Clio Duo (best for small firm practice management), Lexis+ AI (best for LexisNexis users), Westlaw Precision (best for case law research), and Ironclad (best for contract lifecycle management). The right choice depends on your firm size, practice area and whether you prioritise research, drafting or contract management.
For large firms handling high-value transactions and document-intensive work, Harvey can deliver significant ROI — reducing associate hours on due diligence and research substantially. Allen & Overy (now A&O Shearman) reported meaningful efficiency gains after their Harvey deployment. For smaller firms, the cost-benefit calculation is harder to justify compared to more accessible alternatives like Legora or CoCounsel.
