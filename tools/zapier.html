What is Zapier?

Zapier is the world's leading workflow automation platform, connecting 6,000+ apps and enabling businesses to automate repetitive tasks without writing a single line of code. When something happens in one app — a new lead in a form, a payment in Stripe, an email in Gmail — Zapier automatically triggers actions in other apps, eliminating manual data transfer and handoffs.

Its AI features include Zapier AI Actions (which lets AI tools like ChatGPT and Claude trigger real actions in your apps), an AI workflow builder that creates automations from plain English descriptions, and AI-powered data transformation that formats and enriches data as it moves between apps. These features make Zapier one of the most important AI integration layers for modern businesses.

For marketers, Zapier automates lead routing, CRM updates and campaign triggers. For operations teams, it connects project management, finance and communication tools. For ecommerce, it syncs orders, inventory and customer data. With 6,000+ integrations, if you use a software tool in your business, Zapier almost certainly connects to it.

Frequently Asked Questions
Zapier automates repetitive tasks between apps — for example: when a new lead fills out a form, automatically add them to your CRM, send a welcome email and notify your sales team in Slack. It eliminates manual data entry and handoffs between tools. Businesses use it to automate lead routing, order processing, reporting, notifications and hundreds of other workflows.
Zapier has a free plan that includes 100 tasks per month and 5 active Zaps — enough for basic automations. Paid plans start at $19.99/month (Starter) for 750 tasks and multi-step Zaps. Professional plans at $49/month include unlimited premium apps and advanced features. Pricing scales with the number of tasks your automations run each month.
Zapier is better for simplicity and app coverage — it's easier to set up and has more integrations. Make (formerly Integromat) is better for complex, high-volume automations at lower cost — its visual workflow builder handles complex logic more elegantly and is significantly cheaper per task. Most businesses start with Zapier for ease of use and switch to Make if they need to scale volume cost-effectively.
