What is Workday AI?

Workday is the leading enterprise HR and finance platform used by 10,000+ organisations including Netflix, Salesforce and Bank of America. It covers the entire employee lifecycle — from recruitment and onboarding through payroll, performance management, learning and development to workforce analytics and succession planning.

Workday AI is embedded throughout the platform — it uses machine learning to surface candidate recommendations, predict attrition risk, suggest learning content, automate payroll anomaly detection and generate workforce insights. Unlike bolt-on AI tools, Workday's AI works with your actual HR data, making its predictions significantly more accurate and relevant.

For HR leaders and CHROs, Workday provides a single system of record for all people data — eliminating the spreadsheets and disconnected tools that plague enterprise HR. Its analytics capabilities let leaders model workforce scenarios, plan headcount and understand the ROI of HR programmes at a level no other platform matches.

Frequently Asked Questions
Workday is designed for mid-to-large enterprises — typically 500+ employees, though its sweet spot is 1,000+ employees. For smaller companies, the cost and implementation complexity make it impractical. SMBs are better served by tools like Gusto, BambooHR or Rippling. Workday becomes compelling when you need global payroll, complex org structures and advanced workforce analytics.
Workday AI is embedded throughout the platform — it's not a separate module. The AI learns from your organisation's data: your job descriptions, your successful hires, your performance reviews, your attrition patterns. This makes it far more accurate than generic AI tools. It surfaces insights like 'these 15 employees are at high flight risk' or 'this candidate matches your top performer profile' based on your actual data.
