What is Tidio?

Tidio is an AI-powered customer service platform combining live chat, chatbots and email support in one tool — used by 300,000+ businesses. Its standout feature is Lyro AI, a conversational AI that learns from your FAQ content and automatically resolves customer questions without human involvement, typically handling 60-70% of incoming queries.

For small businesses and ecommerce stores, Tidio solves the fundamental problem of needing to be available 24/7 for customer questions without the cost of round-the-clock staff. Lyro AI handles common questions about orders, shipping, returns and product information automatically, escalating to a human agent only when it can't confidently answer.

Tidio integrates natively with Shopify, WooCommerce, WordPress and most major ecommerce and CMS platforms. Its mobile app lets business owners respond to live chat conversations from anywhere. The free plan includes 50 Lyro AI conversations per month and unlimited live chat — making it the most accessible AI customer service tool available for small businesses.

Frequently Asked Questions
Lyro is Tidio's conversational AI that automatically answers customer questions based on your FAQ content and previous conversations. You give it your help documentation and it learns to answer questions in natural language — resolving shipping queries, return policies, product questions and more without human involvement. It handles about 60-70% of typical customer questions, escalating anything it can't answer to a human agent.
Tidio has a free plan that includes unlimited live chat agents, 50 Lyro AI conversations per month and basic chatbot flows. The free plan is enough for small businesses with low support volumes to get started. Paid plans start at $29/month for more Lyro conversations and advanced features. Lyro AI conversation packs can also be purchased separately if you need more volume without upgrading the full plan.
Tidio is better for small businesses — it's significantly more affordable, has a free plan and is easier to set up. Intercom is better for growing SaaS companies that need sophisticated customer lifecycle messaging, product tours and advanced segmentation. Most small businesses don't need Intercom's complexity or price point. Start with Tidio and consider Intercom when you're scaling past 10,000 users.
