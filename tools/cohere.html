What is Cohere?

Cohere is an enterprise AI company focused on building large language models for business use — specifically designed for deployment in private cloud environments where data never leaves the organisation's infrastructure. Used by Oracle, Salesforce and Fujitsu, Cohere serves regulated industries including finance, healthcare and government where data sovereignty is non-negotiable.

Cohere's flagship products are Command (for text generation and summarisation), Embed (for semantic search and retrieval) and Rerank (for improving search result relevance). Together these power enterprise use cases like internal knowledge search, document summarisation, customer support automation and content generation — all deployable on AWS, Azure, Google Cloud or on-premise.

Cohere's RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) capabilities are particularly strong — connecting its Command model to enterprise knowledge bases so the AI answers questions based on your actual company documents rather than general training data. This makes it highly accurate for internal use cases where generic AI knowledge isn't sufficient.

Frequently Asked Questions
Cohere is used by enterprises to build AI-powered search, document summarisation and content generation into their own products and workflows — with data staying in their private cloud. Common use cases include internal knowledge base search, customer support automation, contract analysis, report generation and semantic document classification.
For enterprises with strict data privacy requirements, Cohere wins — its private cloud deployment means data never leaves your infrastructure, unlike OpenAI's API. For capability and model quality on general tasks, OpenAI's GPT-4 leads. Enterprises in regulated industries (finance, healthcare, government) typically choose Cohere; others often use OpenAI's enterprise tier with data processing agreements.
Cohere offers a free trial API with rate-limited access to all models — sufficient for developers to evaluate and build proofs of concept. Production use requires a paid plan; pricing is usage-based and enterprise contracts are negotiated directly. The free trial doesn't expire but has strict rate limits.
