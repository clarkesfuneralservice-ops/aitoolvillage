What is Gusto?

Gusto is a cloud-based payroll, HR and benefits platform built specifically for small and medium-sized businesses. Founded in 2011 (originally as ZenPayroll), Gusto now serves over 300,000 businesses across the US and is consistently ranked as the most user-friendly payroll software available.

For accountants, Gusto is a game-changer. Its accountant portal lets you manage payroll for multiple clients from a single dashboard, with automated tax filings, direct deposit and benefits administration handled entirely by the platform. Gusto files federal, state and local payroll taxes automatically — eliminating one of the most time-consuming tasks in an accounting practice.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does Gusto do?
Gusto is payroll, HR and benefits software for small businesses. It automates payroll calculations, tax filings, direct deposit, employee onboarding, time tracking and benefits administration — all in one platform.
Is Gusto free for accountants?
Yes — Gusto offers a free partner account for accountants and bookkeepers. You get access to manage all your clients at no cost, plus Gusto pays you a revenue share for each client you bring onto the platform.
Does Gusto file payroll taxes automatically?
Yes — this is one of Gusto's biggest selling points. It automatically calculates, files and pays federal, state and local payroll taxes on your behalf. At year end it generates and files W-2s and 1099s automatically.
Does Gusto integrate with QuickBooks and Xero?
Yes — Gusto integrates directly with QuickBooks Online, Xero, FreshBooks, Wave and most major accounting platforms. Payroll journal entries sync automatically, eliminating manual data entry.
Is Gusto available outside the US?
No — Gusto is currently only available to businesses registered in the United States. For international payroll, alternatives like Deel or Remote are better options.
How does Gusto compare to ADP?
Gusto is significantly easier to use and better priced for small businesses (under 50 employees). ADP has more advanced features for larger companies. For most small businesses and their accountants, Gusto is the better choice.
