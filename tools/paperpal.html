What is Paperpal?

Paperpal is an AI writing assistant built specifically for academic and scientific writing — used by over 1 million researchers, students and academics worldwide. Unlike general AI writing tools like Grammarly or ChatGPT, Paperpal is trained on millions of published research papers and understands the specific conventions, tone and structure required in scholarly writing across disciplines.

Its core features include academic grammar and language improvement, paraphrasing that maintains scholarly tone, consistency checks for terminology and abbreviations throughout a document, and a journal submission check that flags common issues before you submit a manuscript. For researchers writing papers for peer-reviewed journals, Paperpal catches the language quality issues that cause desk rejections — grammatical errors, inconsistent terminology, passive voice overuse and non-native English phrasing that reviewers flag.

Paperpal integrates directly into Microsoft Word as an add-in — you get AI assistance within your existing writing workflow without switching tools. There's also a web editor for browser-based writing. The tool supports 25+ languages and is particularly valuable for non-native English speaking researchers who want to write at native-speaker level for international journals.

Who is Paperpal for?

Paperpal is designed for academic researchers submitting papers to peer-reviewed journals, PhD students writing theses and dissertations, university students writing research essays and reports, and non-native English speakers needing academic language assistance. It is specifically not designed for marketing copy, creative writing or general business writing — those use cases are better served by Grammarly or Jasper AI.
