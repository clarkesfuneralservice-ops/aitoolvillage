What is HubSpot?

HubSpot is the world's leading CRM platform for growing businesses — used by 205,000+ customers in 135 countries. It provides a fully integrated suite of marketing, sales, customer service and operations tools built around a central CRM database, giving every team a unified view of every customer interaction across the entire business relationship.

HubSpot's AI features, branded as HubSpot AI, are embedded throughout the platform. AI writing assistants help marketing teams draft emails, landing page copy, social posts and blog content. AI sales tools help reps prioritise leads, write follow-up emails and forecast deal close probability. AI-powered customer service tools route tickets, suggest responses and identify at-risk customers before they churn.

The free CRM is genuinely powerful — unlimited contacts, deal tracking, email integration, basic forms and landing pages, and 15 minutes of calling per month. Unlike Salesforce which is expensive from day one, HubSpot lets businesses start completely free and upgrade specific hubs as their needs grow. This means a startup can use HubSpot for two years before paying anything, then upgrade marketing or sales features as revenue justifies the investment.

HubSpot vs Salesforce

HubSpot is better for small to mid-size businesses that want an easy-to-use, all-in-one platform with strong marketing tools. Salesforce is better for large enterprises needing maximum customisation and a vast ecosystem of integrations. Most businesses under 500 employees get significantly more value from HubSpot at a fraction of the Salesforce cost.

Frequently Asked Questions
HubSpot's free plan includes the full CRM with unlimited contacts, deal tracking, email integration, basic email marketing (2,000 sends/month), 1 landing page, live chat and basic reporting. There's no time limit and no credit card required. Paid plans start at $20/month for the Starter tier and add more email sends, automation, A/B testing and reporting. For most small businesses starting out, the free plan provides everything needed for 1-2 years.
HubSpot is better for companies under 500 employees wanting an easy-to-use all-in-one platform — it's significantly easier to set up, includes strong marketing tools and has a far more accessible starting price. Salesforce is better for large enterprises needing extreme customisation, a massive partner ecosystem and the ability to configure the CRM around very complex sales processes. Moving from HubSpot to Salesforce is easier than starting on Salesforce from day one.
HubSpot AI includes: AI content writer for drafting emails, landing pages, blog posts and social content; AI lead scoring that predicts which contacts are most likely to convert; deal close probability forecasting based on historical data; AI-powered email subject line recommendations; smart send time optimisation; and AI chatbot builder for website and customer service automation. Most AI features are available from the Professional tier upwards.
