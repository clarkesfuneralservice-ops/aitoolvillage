What is Lever?

Lever is an applicant tracking system (ATS) that combines traditional recruitment workflow management with CRM-style relationship features — making it the preferred choice for talent acquisition teams who think of recruiting as an ongoing relationship rather than a transactional process. It's used by 5,000+ companies including Netflix, KPMG and McGraw-Hill.

Its key differentiator from pure ATS platforms like Greenhouse is the Talent CRM layer — candidates who aren't the right fit for a current role can be tagged, nurtured and re-engaged for future openings. This dramatically increases the ROI on sourcing investment: instead of losing great candidates because the timing wasn't right, Lever keeps them warm in your pipeline.

Lever's AI features include candidate scoring that surfaces best-fit applicants, pipeline analytics that identify bottlenecks in your hiring process, and diversity reporting that tracks representation at each stage. Its visual pipeline view gives recruiters and hiring managers a clear, real-time picture of where every candidate stands across all open roles.

Frequently Asked Questions
Greenhouse is better for companies with complex, structured hiring processes and large recruiting teams — its interview kit and scorecard system is more rigorous. Lever is better for companies that prioritise talent relationship management alongside tracking — its CRM layer is superior. Both are strong mid-market ATS platforms; the choice comes down to whether structured hiring or talent pipeline management is your bigger priority.
A Talent CRM (Candidate Relationship Management) works like a sales CRM but for candidates — it lets recruiters maintain relationships with people who weren't hired but might be right for future roles. Without a CRM, most companies lose great candidates because the timing wasn't right. With Lever's CRM, a candidate you meet at a conference or a strong applicant who took another offer stays in your pipeline for the next relevant opening.
Lever is designed for companies with dedicated recruiting teams — typically 100+ employees with at least one full-time recruiter. Small businesses with occasional hiring needs are better served by simpler tools like Workable or even LinkedIn's built-in recruiting tools. Lever's full value comes when you're hiring consistently enough to benefit from pipeline building and advanced analytics.
