What is Polymer?

Polymer is a no-code business intelligence platform that transforms spreadsheets, databases and data files into interactive dashboards with AI-powered natural language querying. Connect your Google Sheets, Excel file, Airtable base, CSV or database, and Polymer automatically creates a searchable, filterable dashboard where team members can explore data by typing questions rather than building reports manually.

Polymer's AI layer allows users to query their data in plain English — asking questions like 'which sales rep had the highest close rate last month' or 'show me customer signups by country over the past 6 months' — and receiving instant chart visualisations in response. This self-service analytics capability means non-technical team members can get data answers without waiting for a data analyst to build a custom report.

The platform is particularly popular with marketing teams analysing campaign performance, e-commerce teams exploring sales data, operations teams tracking KPIs and startups that want BI capabilities without the cost and complexity of Tableau or Power BI. Polymer's free plan is genuinely useful for small datasets, and the paid plans are significantly more affordable than enterprise BI tools.

Polymer vs Tableau vs Julius AI

Tableau is the enterprise standard — more powerful and more expensive. Julius AI is better for conversational data analysis and complex statistical queries. Polymer sits in the middle — it's better than Julius AI for creating persistent shared dashboards that teams access regularly, but less powerful than Tableau for complex multi-source enterprise BI. For teams that need shareable, always-available dashboards from their existing spreadsheets, Polymer is the sweet spot.

Frequently Asked Questions
Polymer has a free plan that includes 1 data source connection, basic visualisations and limited rows of data — enough to evaluate the platform on a real dataset. Paid plans start at $10/month for more data sources, larger datasets, more visualisation types and team sharing features. The free plan is a genuine starting point for small teams or individuals with modest data analysis needs.
Google Looker Studio (formerly Data Studio) is completely free and has excellent Google product integrations. Polymer has better natural language querying, a cleaner interface and easier setup for non-technical users. For teams already in the Google ecosystem (Google Sheets, Google Analytics), Looker Studio is worth trying first given its zero cost. Polymer is better when you want AI-powered natural language querying and a more guided data exploration experience.
Polymer connects to Google Sheets, Microsoft Excel, CSV files, Airtable, PostgreSQL, MySQL, MongoDB and other common data sources. New integrations are added regularly. For most business teams whose data lives in spreadsheets or common databases, Polymer covers the main use cases. For organisations needing connections to enterprise data warehouses like Snowflake or BigQuery at scale, Tableau or Power BI are better suited.
