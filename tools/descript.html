What is Descript?

Descript is an AI-powered video and podcast editing platform built around a revolutionary concept — instead of editing a timeline with clips and waveforms, you edit the automatically generated transcript. Delete a word from the transcript and the corresponding audio and video are removed. Cut a paragraph and that section disappears from the recording. For creators who find traditional video editing intimidating, this text-based approach makes editing as intuitive as editing a document.

Used by 500,000+ podcasters, YouTubers, course creators and video marketers, Descript has become the go-to tool for creators who want to produce professional video and audio content without learning complex editing software. Its AI features go far beyond transcription — Overdub can clone your voice so you can correct mistakes by typing without re-recording, Studio Sound removes background noise and room echo automatically, and Filler Word Removal identifies and removes "um", "uh" and "like" with a single click.

Descript's recent updates have added AI-powered clip creation that identifies the most shareable moments in long recordings for social media, AI eye contact correction that makes it appear you're looking at the camera even when reading a script, and a full screen recording tool for creating tutorials and demos. This evolution from editor to complete content creation platform makes Descript one of the most versatile tools available for video creators.

Who is Descript best for?

Descript is ideal for podcasters who want to edit episodes quickly without timeline editing, YouTubers who create talking-head content and want to remove mistakes and dead air efficiently, course creators producing educational video content, marketers creating video ads and social content, and anyone who records presentations or interviews and needs to clean them up professionally without hiring an editor.

Frequently Asked Questions
When you import a video or audio file into Descript, it automatically transcribes the content and displays the transcript alongside your media. The transcript is fully synced to the recording — every word is linked to its corresponding timestamp. When you select and delete text in the transcript, Descript removes that exact portion of the audio and video. You can also highlight text and cut, copy or rearrange it to restructure your recording. This makes editing as intuitive as editing a text document.
Descript has a free plan that includes 1 hour of AI transcription per month, text-based editing, filler word removal and basic export. Core AI features like Overdub voice cloning, Studio Sound audio enhancement and eye contact correction require the Hobbyist plan at $24/month. The free plan is enough to evaluate Descript's editing approach and decide if it suits your workflow before committing to a subscription.
Descript's AI transcription is very accurate for clear English speech — typically 95%+ accuracy for good-quality recordings with minimal background noise. Accuracy decreases with strong accents, multiple overlapping speakers, technical jargon and noisy environments. The transcription is editable, so any errors can be corrected manually. For non-English content, accuracy varies by language — check Descript's current language support list for the languages relevant to your content.
Use Descript if you're a content creator, podcaster or YouTuber who wants to edit quickly without learning complex editing software — its text-based approach makes basic editing fast and its AI features save significant time. Use Premiere Pro if you're a professional video editor who needs precise multi-track editing, colour grading, complex transitions and effects, or are producing broadcast-quality content. Most serious creators use Descript for rough cuts and cleanup, then export to Premiere Pro for final polish if needed.
Descript's Overdub voice cloning requires you to record a training script (about 10 minutes of your voice). The resulting voice clone is typically quite accurate for correcting small mistakes — replacing a word or sentence — but can sound slightly robotic for longer passages. It works best for fixing individual words or short phrases where re-recording would be time-consuming. Voice clone quality varies by voice type — some voices clone better than others. Available on Hobbyist and Creator plans.
