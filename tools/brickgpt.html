What is BrickGPT?

BrickGPT is an AI-powered tool that solves one of the most frustrating problems in the LEGO hobby — reverse-engineering a completed brick model back into buildable instructions. You photograph a finished build, upload the image, and BrickGPT's AI analyses the structure and generates clear, step-by-step assembly instructions along with a short video showing the build sequence from start to finish.

This is genuinely novel. Previously, documenting a custom LEGO creation (known in the community as a MOC — My Own Creation) required either expensive specialised software like BrickLink Studio or hours of manual photography and documentation. BrickGPT compresses this process to minutes, making it accessible to hobbyists, parents, educators and anyone who builds with LEGO without requiring technical design software skills.

The tool has clear applications across several audiences. Parents can photograph a completed model and hand their child instructions to rebuild it later. Educators can document classroom builds. MOC creators can generate shareable instructions for their designs. Toy marketers and brand agencies can produce instruction assets quickly for product demonstrations or promotional content.

Who is BrickGPT for?

BrickGPT is designed for LEGO hobbyists and MOC creators who want to document and share their builds, parents who want to help children rebuild completed models, educators using LEGO in classroom settings, and toy marketers or brand agencies that need to produce instruction content for brick-compatible products. At $49/month for paid plans, it's most cost-effective for professional or commercial use cases.

Frequently Asked Questions
BrickGPT uses computer vision AI to analyse the structure of a brick model from your uploaded photo — identifying individual bricks, their positions, colours and connections. The AI then reconstructs the likely build sequence and outputs it as numbered step-by-step instructions with visual diagrams, plus generates a short video showing the assembly process. The accuracy is highest for clear photos with good lighting and models of moderate complexity.
BrickGPT offers a freemium model — there is a free tier that lets you evaluate the quality of instructions generated from your photos before committing to a paid plan. Paid plans start at $49/month, which is positioned for professional or commercial users such as toy marketers, educators running programmes at scale or active MOC creators who publish instructions regularly. Check the BrickGPT website for current plan details.
Accuracy depends on photo quality and model complexity. For simpler, well-lit models with clear brick visibility, BrickGPT generates instructions that are largely correct and buildable with minor adjustments. For very complex MOCs with many obscured bricks or unusual techniques, the instructions may require more manual editing. The AI improves with clearer, multi-angle photos. As with all new AI tools, treating the output as a strong first draft rather than a finished product gives the best results.
BrickGPT delivers the most value for: toy marketers and brand agencies needing instruction assets quickly for brick-compatible products; active MOC creators in the LEGO community who regularly document and share their builds; educators running LEGO-based programmes who want to document classroom builds; and parents who want rebuild instructions for custom models their children have made. Casual occasional builders may find the $49/month cost hard to justify versus the free tier.
