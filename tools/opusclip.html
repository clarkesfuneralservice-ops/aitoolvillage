What is OpusClip?

OpusClip is an AI-powered video repurposing tool that automatically identifies the most engaging moments in long-form videos and transforms them into short clips optimised for TikTok, YouTube Shorts and Instagram Reels. It's used by 10 million+ creators to get more content from every long video they produce without manual editing.

The AI analyses your video for 'hooks' — moments that are likely to capture attention and generate engagement based on speech patterns, content density and viewer retention data from millions of viral videos. It then automatically cuts these moments into clips, adds animated captions and arranges them in the optimal format for each platform. The whole process takes minutes rather than hours.

OpusClip also includes Clip Score — a virality prediction score for each generated clip based on the AI's analysis of what makes short-form content perform. This lets creators prioritise which clips to post rather than testing everything. For podcasters, YouTubers and anyone producing long-form video content, OpusClip multiplies content output without multiplying editing time.

Frequently Asked Questions
OpusClip's AI analyses the video transcript and audio for specific signals that correlate with viral short-form content — strong opening hooks, high-energy delivery, quotable statements and narrative completeness. It's trained on data from millions of viral clips to understand what makes short-form content perform. The Clip Score gives each suggested clip a predicted virality rating so you can prioritise the best ones.
OpusClip has a free plan that includes 60 minutes of video processing per month — enough for most creators to produce several short clips from a long video. The Pro plan at $15/month increases this limit significantly and adds advanced features including multi-speaker detection, custom templates and priority processing. The free plan is enough to fully evaluate whether OpusClip fits your workflow.
OpusClip is better if you want fully automated clip creation — paste a link and get clips in minutes with minimal involvement. Descript gives you more control — you edit the transcript to edit the video, which is better for precise, intentional clip creation. For high volume repurposing where speed matters most, OpusClip wins. For crafted, specific clips where quality control matters more, Descript is better.
