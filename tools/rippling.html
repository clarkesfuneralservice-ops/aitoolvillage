What is Rippling?

Rippling is the only platform that unifies HR, IT and Finance in a single system — and the automation this enables is genuinely remarkable. When you hire someone, Rippling automatically sets up their payroll, enrolls them in benefits, provisions their laptop, creates their email account, grants access to every app they need (Slack, Salesforce, GitHub, etc.) and sends their offer letter — all triggered by a single hire action. Offboarding works the same way in reverse.

Founded in 2016 by Parker Conrad (also founder of Zenefits), Rippling has grown to serve 10,000+ companies and is valued at $13.5 billion. Its modular architecture means you can start with just payroll or just HR and add modules as you grow — you're never locked into buying the whole platform upfront.

For fast-growing companies, Rippling's compliance automation is particularly valuable — it automatically applies the right payroll taxes, labour laws and benefits rules based on where each employee is located. As you hire across different states or countries, Rippling handles the compliance complexity that would otherwise require a dedicated HR compliance team.

Frequently Asked Questions
Rippling's unique differentiator is combining HR and IT management in one platform. When you hire someone, Rippling simultaneously sets up payroll, benefits AND IT — provisioning their laptop, creating their email, granting Slack access, setting up their GitHub account etc. No other platform does this. For companies that value operational efficiency and have significant IT management needs alongside HR, this integration saves enormous amounts of time.
Gusto is better for small businesses that primarily need payroll and basic HR — it's simpler, cheaper and has better customer support. Rippling is better for fast-growing companies that need to scale quickly, manage IT alongside HR, or hire internationally. The crossover point is typically around 20-50 employees — below that, Gusto usually wins on simplicity and price; above that, Rippling's power becomes increasingly valuable.
Rippling uses modular pricing — you pay per user per month for each module you use. The core platform starts at $8/user/month. Payroll, benefits, IT management and other modules are priced separately. A typical small business using payroll, HR and basic IT management might pay $15-25/user/month total. Enterprise pricing is custom. Unlike Gusto, there's no flat-rate option — you build your own bundle.
