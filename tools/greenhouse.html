What is Greenhouse?

Greenhouse is the leading applicant tracking system for high-growth technology and professional services companies — used by 7,500+ organisations including Airbnb, DoorDash, HubSpot and Wayfair. It's built around structured hiring: every role has a defined interview plan, every interviewer has a specific focus area and every candidate is scored against the same criteria, ensuring hiring decisions are consistent, fair and data-driven.

Its AI capabilities include candidate scoring that surfaces the best-fit applicants from large volumes of applications, pipeline analytics that identify where top candidates drop off and offer insights into sourcing channel effectiveness, and diversity tools that track representation at every stage and flag potential bias patterns in the hiring process.

Greenhouse's interview kit system is its most distinctive feature — before any interview, interviewers receive a structured guide with specific questions for their focus area and a scorecard to complete immediately after. This means hiring decisions are based on consistent data across all candidates rather than gut feeling, dramatically improving hiring quality and reducing the influence of unconscious bias.

Frequently Asked Questions
High-growth companies choose Greenhouse because it enforces structured hiring — every role has a defined process, every interviewer has a specific focus and every candidate is scored consistently. As companies scale from 50 to 500 to 5,000 employees, unstructured hiring breaks down and leads to inconsistent quality. Greenhouse builds the process rigour that scales, resulting in better hires and less regretted attrition.
Greenhouse AI analyses incoming applications against your job description, required qualifications and historical hiring data to score candidates by predicted fit. It surfaces the highest-scoring applicants at the top of your pipeline rather than showing applications in chronological order. The AI also analyses your pipeline to identify patterns — for example, which sourcing channels produce candidates who advance furthest, or at which stage you're losing the most qualified applicants.
Choose Greenhouse if your priority is structured, rigorous hiring with strong interview consistency and analytics — it's better for companies with large recruiting teams and complex hiring processes. Choose Lever if you want stronger talent relationship management and CRM features alongside ATS functionality. Both are excellent — the decision comes down to whether hiring rigour (Greenhouse) or talent pipeline building (Lever) matters more to your organisation.
