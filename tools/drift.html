What is Drift?

Drift is a conversational marketing and sales platform that uses AI-powered chatbots to engage website visitors in real-time personalised conversations — qualifying leads, answering questions and booking meetings directly into sales reps' calendars, all without human involvement. Used by 50,000+ businesses including Zendesk, Marketo and MongoDB, Drift has pioneered the conversational marketing category.

Traditional B2B lead generation relies on forms — a prospect fills in a form, gets put into a nurture sequence, and eventually gets contacted by a sales rep days later. By that point, their interest has often cooled. Drift replaces this friction-heavy process with immediate, personalised conversation — when a target account visits your website, Drift recognises them, greets them by name and connects them with the right sales rep instantly.

Drift's AI Conversation Cloud uses machine learning trained on millions of B2B sales conversations to qualify prospects, answer product questions and handle objections in natural language. The AI identifies buying intent signals — pages visited, time on site, company size — and escalates high-value prospects to human reps in real time via Slack or email alerts. For enterprise B2B companies with long sales cycles, this acceleration can translate directly into significant revenue impact.

Drift vs traditional live chat

Traditional live chat tools like Intercom and Zendesk Chat require human agents to respond. Drift's AI handles conversations autonomously at any time of day, only escalating to humans when necessary. This means every website visitor gets an immediate, personalised response regardless of when they visit — not just during business hours when agents are available.

Frequently Asked Questions
Drift is primarily used by B2B companies to engage website visitors in real-time conversations, qualify leads automatically and book meetings for sales teams — without requiring live agents. It's also used for account-based marketing (personalising experiences for target accounts), reducing friction in the buying process by answering product questions instantly, and accelerating sales cycles by connecting high-intent prospects with sales reps immediately.
Drift is better for B2B sales and revenue-focused conversational marketing — its account-based features, meeting booking and revenue attribution are superior. Intercom is better for customer support and product-focused messaging — its help desk, knowledge base and user onboarding features are stronger. Most enterprise B2B companies that can afford both use Drift for marketing/sales and Intercom for customer support.
Drift uses custom pricing based on company size, website traffic and features needed. There is no published pricing — you need to contact their sales team for a quote. Based on market data, plans typically start around $2,500/month for small teams and scale significantly for enterprise. Drift is not suitable for companies under $5M ARR where the ROI is difficult to justify at this price point.
