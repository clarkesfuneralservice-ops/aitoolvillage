What is Clay?

Clay is an AI-powered data enrichment and prospecting platform that lets sales and growth teams build hyper-targeted lead lists by combining data from 50+ sources in a single spreadsheet-like interface. Rather than paying for multiple data tools separately (LinkedIn Sales Navigator, Apollo, Clearbit, Hunter.io), Clay aggregates them all and uses AI to enrich, research and score leads automatically.

The core workflow: import a list of target companies or contacts, run Clay's AI enrichment to fill in missing data (email addresses, LinkedIn profiles, company funding, tech stack, recent news), then use Clay's AI — called Claygent — to research each prospect and write personalised outreach at scale. The result is highly targeted, personalised cold outreach built from real-time data without hours of manual research.

Clay has become popular with growth operators, RevOps teams and ambitious SDRs who want to build outbound campaigns that feel personal despite being automated — combining the breadth of data enrichment with the personalisation of AI writing.

Who is Clay for?

B2B sales teams doing outbound prospecting, growth operators building GTM workflows, RevOps teams automating lead enrichment, and agencies managing outbound campaigns for multiple clients. Clay has a steep learning curve — it rewards technical users who invest time in building workflows.

Frequently Asked Questions
Clay is used for B2B prospecting and lead enrichment — building targeted lists of potential customers with verified contact details, company data and AI-generated personalised outreach. Sales teams use it to automate the research and personalisation that would otherwise take hours per prospect, enabling high-quality outbound at scale.
Clay is worth it for sales teams doing significant outbound prospecting who currently pay for multiple data tools separately. If you're paying for LinkedIn Sales Navigator, Apollo, Clearbit and Hunter individually, Clay likely replaces most of that spend at comparable or lower cost while adding AI enrichment on top. For teams doing light prospecting, simpler tools like Apollo.io are more cost-effective.
Each enrichment action in Clay costs credits — looking up an email address, running an AI research task or calling a data provider each costs a certain number of credits. Clay's waterfall enrichment is credit-efficient because it only moves to the next provider if the previous one doesn't find data. The Starter plan includes 2,000 credits per month, which covers moderate enrichment volumes — heavier workflows require higher-tier plans.
Apollo.io is better for teams wanting an all-in-one prospecting and sequencing tool that's simpler to set up and more affordable. Clay is better for technical sales and growth teams who want maximum flexibility, the ability to combine many data sources, and AI-powered personalisation at scale. Many teams use Apollo for sequencing and Clay for enrichment, pushing data from Clay into Apollo.
