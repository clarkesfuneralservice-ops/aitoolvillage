How to Choose

For everyday and personal use

Google Translate is free, supports 133 languages and includes camera translation for signs and menus plus real-time conversation mode — the most accessible option for travel and casual use.

For business documents and professional content

DeepL produces noticeably more natural translations for the 30+ languages it supports — particularly strong for European language pairs and content where tone matters.

For Microsoft 365 organisations

Microsoft Translator integrates directly into Outlook, Teams and Word, making it the path of least resistance for businesses already on the Microsoft ecosystem.

For website and app localisation

Crowdin is popular with software teams for managing translation strings across many languages, while Smartling and Phrase serve larger enterprise localisation programmes with workflow management on top of AI translation.
