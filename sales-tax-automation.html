Frequently Asked Questions
Sales tax automation means using software to automatically calculate the correct sales tax rate for every transaction based on the buyer's location, collect it at checkout, track your liability across states and file returns on your behalf. Without automation, you'd need to manually look up rates for every state, track what you've collected and file returns in each state every month or quarter — a major compliance burden for growing businesses.
If you're selling online across multiple US states, sales tax automation is almost essential. Since the 2018 South Dakota v. Wayfair Supreme Court ruling, businesses can owe sales tax in states where they have no physical presence — based purely on sales volume. Most ecommerce businesses are now required to collect sales tax in multiple states, making manual compliance practically impossible at scale.
Economic nexus means you have a sales tax obligation in a state based on your sales volume there — even without a physical location. Most states set the threshold at $100,000 in sales or 200 transactions in a calendar year. Once you cross that threshold, you must register, collect and remit sales tax in that state. Sales tax automation tools monitor these thresholds and alert you before you're non-compliant.
TaxJar is better for most small and mid-sized ecommerce businesses — it's easier to set up, more affordable and covers all US state filings well. Avalara is better for enterprise businesses with complex multi-state or international needs, or those already using SAP/Oracle ERP systems. Most businesses with under $10M in revenue will find TaxJar sufficient; above that or with international operations, Avalara is worth evaluating.
Shopify Tax is free for the first $100K in US annual sales — the best free option for new Shopify stores. TaxJar starts at $19/month and covers most ecommerce use cases. Stripe Tax charges 0.5% per transaction with no monthly fee, which is cheapest for low-volume businesses. For businesses doing significant volume, TaxJar's flat monthly fee is usually more cost-effective than percentage-based pricing.
